WHAT are orthodontics?

Orthodontics is a dental specialty focused on aligning your bite and straightening your teeth. Common orthodontic treatments include traditional braces, clear aligners and removable retainers. Orthodontics therefore describes the practice of straightening misaligned teeth or malocclusions.

WHO might need orthodontic treatment?

People who have misaligned teeth or jaws can benefit from orthodontic treatment. You might need orthodontic treatment if you have:

  • Crowded or crooked teeth.
  • Overbite or Underbite.
  • Gaps between teeth.
  • Misaligned jaw.
  • Openbite or Crossbite
  • Speech or chewing difficulties due to dental misalignment.

ADDITIONAL INFORMATIONS

Orthodontic treatments, while generally safe and effective, can also carry potential risks and complications. Possible risks include:

  • Discomfort or pain from braces or aligners.
  • Relapse of teeth to their original position after treatment.
  • Tooth decay or gum disease if oral hygiene is not maintained.
  • Root resorption (shortening of tooth roots).
  • Difficulty in eating or speaking initially.

Orthodontic treatments offer a wide range of advantages. They can:

  • Improve the alignment and appearance of your teeth and smile.
  • Enhance your bite, making it easier to chew and speak properly.
  • Help alleviate issues related to misaligned teeth, such as jaw pain and headaches.
  • Promote better oral health by making teeth easier to clean and reducing the risk of decay and gum disease.

Adjusting to orthodontic treatments varies, but most people get used to braces or aligners within a few days to a week. During this initial period, you might feel some discomfort or soreness, but over-the-counter pain relievers can help manage this.


Orthodontic treatments require regular check-ups to adjust the braces or get new aligners. The total treatment time can range from several months to a few years, depending on how complex your case is. After your treatment is finished, it's important to wear retainers as your orthodontist recommends. This will help keep your teeth in their new positions, preventing them from shifting back.
