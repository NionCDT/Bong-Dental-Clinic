ORAL SURGERY

Smile! Its Free!

WHAT are oral surgeries?

Oral surgeries fix problems in the mouth and jaw. Common procedures include removing teeth, placing dental implants, correcting jaw alignment, adding bone, treating gum disease, performing root canals, removing abnormal growths, fixing cleft lips and palates, treating jaw joint issues, and addressing sleep apnea. Specialized dental surgeons usually do these surgeries.

HOW do oral surgeries work?

Oral surgeries vary widely but generally follow similar steps: preparation, procedure, and recovery. For tooth extractions, local anesthesia is used, and the tooth is removed with forceps or surgically if more complex.Dental implants involve placing a metal post in the jawbone, which heals over months before attaching a custom crown. Corrective jaw surgeries require repositioning bones under general anesthesia with a hospital stay for recovery.

WHO might need an oral surgery?

Oral surgery may be necessary for various reasons and can be recommended by dentists or specialists such as an oral surgeon. Common reasons to perform include:


  • Tooth Loss.
  • Misaligned Jaw.
  • Dental Implants.
  • Facial Trauma.
  • Cleft Lip and Palate.
  • Treatment of Oral Pathologies.

ADDITIONAL INFORMATIONS

Like any surgical procedure, oral surgery carries some risks and potential complications, although they are generally rare. Some of these risks include:

  • Pain and Swelling.
  • Bleeding.
  • Infection.
  • Nerve Damage.
  • Dry Socket.

Oral surgery offers numerous benefits, depending on the specific procedure and the individual's needs. Some of these benefits include:

  • Pain Relief.
  • Improved Oral Health.
  • Enhanced Aesthetics.
  • Better Bite and Chewing Function.
  • Treatment of Trauma

Recovery times after oral surgery vary depending on the procedure. Simple tooth extractions usually take about 1 to 2 weeks for complete healing. Wisdom teeth removal typically involves 3 to 5 days of initial recovery, with full healing in 1 to 2 weeks. Dental implants require about 1 to 2 weeks for initial healing, but full integration with the jawbone may take several months. Orthognathic surgery, which corrects jaw misalignment, entails 2 to 4 weeks for initial healing and several months to a year for full recovery.

The experience of pain during oral surgery can vary depending on factors such as the type of procedure, individual pain tolerance, and the use of anesthesia or sedation. While some discomfort or mild pain is common after oral surgery, modern techniques and medications are used to manage and minimize this discomfort effectively.

Yes, oral surgeries are generally safe when performed by qualified oral surgeons in appropriate medical settings. Surgeons undergo extensive training to minimize risks, and advancements in anesthesia and surgical techniques further enhance safety. Patients should communicate openly about their medical history and concerns to tailor treatment plans and reduce risks.
